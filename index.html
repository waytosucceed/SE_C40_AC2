Word Problems on Speed, Distance, and Time involving Ratio, Fraction, and Percentage Concepts

Example (Ratio-Based):
Two runners, X and Y, start a race at the same time. The ratio of their speeds is 3:2. If X finishes a 24 km race in 2 hours, how long will Y take to complete the same race?
Explanation: Since, we know the distance covered by X in 2 hours, we first find the speed of X:

Speed of X = DistanceTime = 24 km2 hours = 12km/h

Next, use the speed ratio (X:Y = 3:2) to find Y’s speed:

Example (Fraction-Based):
A bus covers 5/8 of a journey at 40 km/h and the remaining 3/8 at 60 km/h. If the total distance is 160 km, how much time does the bus take?
Explanation: Distance covered at 40 km/h:

58 × 160 k = 100 km

Time taken for this part:

Time = DistanceSpeed = 10040 = 2.5 hours

Distance covered at 60 km/h:

38 × 160 = 60 km

Time taken for this part:

Time = DistanceSpeed = 6060 = 1 hour

Therefore, the total time taken by the bus to cover a distance of 160 km = 2.5 hours + 1 hour = 3.5 hours

Example (Percentage-Based):
A train takes 2 hours to travel 120 km at its usual speed. If its speed is reduced by 20%, how much extra time will it take?
Explanation: First we find the original speed:

Speed = DistanceTime = 120 km2 hours = 60 km/h

New speed after 20% reduction:

60 − (20% of 60) = 60 − 12 = 48 km/h

Time taken with reduced speed:

Time = DistanceSpeed = 120 km48 km/h = 2.5 hours

Thus, the extra time bus will take with the reduced speed:

2.5 hours − 2 = 0.5 hours = 0.5 × 60 = 30 minutes
